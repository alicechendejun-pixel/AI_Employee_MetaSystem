# Stock Research Executor

## Goal

Produce a sourced stock-research report that separates verified facts, analysis and scenarios. Prefer a brief from `stock-question-refiner`.

## Phase 1: Business Foundation

Document products, revenue and profit mix, customers, geography, value-chain position and recent strategic changes.

## Phase 2: Industry and Cycle

Assess industry structure, concentration, supply and demand, pricing, cycle stage, policy exposure, competitors and substitutes.

## Phase 3: Business Economics

Explain the profit engine, pricing power, recurring revenue, unit economics where available, capital intensity and major operating drivers.

## Phase 4: Financial Quality

Review at least three years when available:

- Revenue, earnings and margins.
- Operating cash flow and free cash flow.
- ROE and ROIC.
- Debt, liquidity and interest burden.
- Receivables, inventory and accruals.
- Dilution and non-recurring items.

Mandatory checks:

- Operating cash flow versus net income.
- Receivables growth versus revenue growth.
- Inventory growth versus revenue growth.
- Capital expenditure versus depreciation.
- Organic growth versus acquisition-driven growth.

## Phase 5: Ownership and Governance

Review controlling shareholders, voting rights, board independence, management incentives, related-party transactions, insider activity, buybacks, placements, lock-ups and capital allocation.

## Phase 6: Market Disagreement

Build independent bull and bear cases. Identify the main disputed assumptions and the future data that can resolve them. Do not weaken the bear case to support a preferred conclusion.

## Phase 7: Moat and Valuation

Rate moat from 0 to 5 with evidence. Use methods suitable for the business and provide bear, base and bull scenarios. State revenue, margin, capital intensity, discount-rate and terminal assumptions. Explain what expectations are already embedded in the current price.

## Phase 8: Synthesis

Return:

- One-paragraph thesis.
- Strongest confirming evidence.
- Strongest counter-thesis.
- Critical risks.
- Valuation range and assumptions.
- Monitoring checklist.
- GREEN / YELLOW / RED / DATA_BLOCKED.

## Source Grades

- A: regulatory filings, exchange notices, government data and audited reports.
- B: company investor materials, reputable industry data and identified analyst research.
- C: credible journalism and expert commentary.
- D: blogs and social-media leads requiring verification.
- E: unsupported rumor.

Every material factual claim needs a source and date. Prefer A and B sources.

## Contradictions

When sources disagree:

1. Record both claims.
2. Check definitions and periods.
3. Prefer primary filings.
4. State unresolved gaps.
5. Reduce confidence rather than inventing a reconciliation.

## Required Deliverables

- Executive summary.
- Eight research phases.
- Key financial table.
- Peer comparison.
- Bull and bear matrix.
- Scenario valuation.
- Risk register.
- Monitoring checklist with thresholds and cadence.
- Source bibliography and module trace.
